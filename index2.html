Olongapo City

Olongapo, officially the City of Olongapo and often referred to as Olongapo City, is a highly urbanized city located in the province of Zambales, Philippines. It has a population of 221,178 people according to the 2010 census. Along with the town of Subic, it comprises Metro Olongapo, one of the twelve metropolitan areas in the Philippines.

Description/Short history

Olongapo City is located in the province of Zambales, Philippines. It is one of the twelve metropolitan areas in the Philippines and has 221, 178 population according to the 2010 census. Olongapo is situated at the Southern entry point of Zambales and the Northwestern interior of the Subic Bay area. It is approximately 127 kilometres north of Manila. The city is characterized by rugged and mountainous terrain. The development of the city is limited because of its peculiar geographic location, Zambalesmountains on its three sides, Bataan and Subic Bay at its base. From late October through February, the weather in tropical Olongapo is relatively cool. Temperature increase slightly from March to May. From June and continues through October will be rainy season. Main languages in the city are Tagalog, Kapampangan, Ilocano, Sambal, Cebuano and Taglish (Tagalog and English). Majority of the people of Olongapo are Roman Catholics. Olongapo City is an Independent city from the province of Zambales, only the city government officials are voted by the residents of the city. The provincial government has no political jurisdiction over local transactions of the city government. The airport in Olongapo City is the Subic Bay International airport, the airport serving the immediate area of the Subic Bay Freeport Zone and the general area of the city in the Philippines. This is used to be the Naval Air Station Cubi Point of the U.S Navy. Its airport has 2 gates. Presently, it was proposed to be converted into a logisitics hub. The busiest, largest and historical and most important of Ports in the Philippines is the Port of Subic. It is being managed by the Subic Bay Metropolitan Authority or SBMA. Olongapo City was the last piece of the Philippine territory surrendered by the US to country in 1950s. Acres of land with electrical, telephone and water utilities was relinquished to Philippine Secretary of Foreign Affairs Felberto Serrano. In June 1, 1966, Olongapo was reconverted to a chartered city. The largest United States Navy installation in the Pacific at the time is the adjacent U.S Naval Base Subic Bay. It is being visited by 215 ships per month as Vietnam War activity peaked in 1967. After the bombing in 1945 by the Japanese and shortly after war was over, Olongapo was one of the principal navy bases retained by the U.S. Starting from scratch, Olongapo was able to survive and gradually it evolved into a better community with has established new business, planned for housing projects and restored civic facilities. The city is known for its innovative methods of urban management in the 1980s in addressing crime and cleanliness.
